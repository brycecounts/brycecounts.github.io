Welcome to Bryce's Webpage

I am curious about the nature of intelligence and how it manifests across diverse bodies and systems, from brains to cells. Most of my work is between computational neuroscience, AI and bioinformatics. One of my major projects over the last few years has been trying to use applied category theory to model and understand the structural aspects of neural representation. I am also curious whether this approach can be pushed beyond neural representation to account for intelligence generally by providing the toolkit to build a comprehensive theory. Some of my related projects involve trying to explore the possibility of representation and computation in systems that do not directly involve neurons, or are at lower levels of organization, such as coordination complexes and dendrites.
I am a member of the Digital Biology Lab at the University of Missouri, where I am working on an AI architecture that can classify protein folds according to neurodegenerative disease. To the extent it is possible, one major goal of mine is to try and bridge insights from advances in neural representation to help build AI systems that are capable of capturing the clinical features relevant for medical research.
I also organize a small research group called the Mind and Computation Research Group where we discuss a range of topics related to AI and computation. We are happy to have people join, so if you are interested, please send me an email! I am also always happy to collaborate with like-minded researchers curious about area of overlap. Please get in touch if you would like to chat or discuss a potential collaboration.
